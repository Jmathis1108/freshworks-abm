The Challenge

When your product is perfect
but your support loses the subscriber

Netflix invests billions in content to earn and keep subscribers. A single bad support interaction can undo that investment in minutes. The CX team has to be as good as the product.

Billing friction is the leading cause of avoidable churn

A failed payment, an unexpected charge, or a confusing plan change sends subscribers to the cancel page before they think to contact support. The window to save them is short. Slow, impersonal support closes it permanently.

190 countries means 190 different support expectations

A subscriber in Brazil expects WhatsApp. One in Japan expects formal, precise language. One in Germany has consumer rights that shape what you can and cannot offer. Generic support treats everyone the same and satisfies no one fully.

Streaming issues feel personal even when they are technical

A subscriber who cannot watch a show they have been anticipating all week is emotionally invested. A ticket number and a three-day wait makes it worse. Fast, empathetic resolution in the moment is the only acceptable outcome.

You cannot retain what you cannot see coming

Most churn is predictable. Subscribers send signals: repeated failed logins, billing page visits, slower app engagement, support tickets that escalate in tone. Without a system that reads those signals in real time, by the time they cancel, it is already too late.

Meet Freddy AI

AI that turns
cancel intent into loyalty

Most AI support tools close tickets. Freddy AI reads context. When a Netflix subscriber opens a chat about a billing issue, Freddy does not just resolve the ticket. It checks whether this is a high-value long-term subscriber, whether this is their first issue or their third this quarter, and whether a courtesy gesture is warranted.

That intelligence is what turns a frustrated billing complaint into a retained subscriber who feels genuinely cared for. Freddy handles the resolution. The goodwill is what stays.

People-first AI means subscribers feel like the company noticed them as individuals, not ticket numbers. That is what Netflix's brand promises. Freddy AI is how support delivers on it at scale.
